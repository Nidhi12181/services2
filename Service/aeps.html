Aadhaar Enabled Payment System

What is AePS?
The Aadhaar Enabled Payment System (AePS) is a secure and simple platform introduced by NPCI, allowing users to perform transactions using their Aadhaar number and biometric authentication. It enables seamless bank-to-bank transfers without needing bank details, relying on biometric verification such as fingerprints or iris scans.

Banking Services Offered
AePS supports essential banking services, including balance inquiry, cash deposit, cash withdrawal, mini statements, and fund transfers. Its interoperability allows users to access their bank accounts from any micro-ATM or business correspondent, regardless of the bank they belong to.

How to Use AePS
To use AePS, a person needs their Aadhaar number linked to a bank account and must authenticate transactions using their biometrics. The system is especially beneficial in rural and remote areas where traditional banking infrastructure is limited, promoting financial inclusion.

Convenience and Security
AePS eliminates the need for a physical card or smartphone, making transactions simple by using just Aadhaar and biometric verification. The system ensures that only the rightful account holder can perform transactions, enhancing security.

Eligibility and Transaction Limits
Any individual with an Aadhaar-linked bank account is eligible for AePS services. Banks or regulatory authorities may impose transaction limits to ensure safe and responsible usage.

Widespread Accessibility
AePS is supported by various banks, micro-ATMs, and business correspondents, making it widely accessible across India. Users can conduct real-time transactions quickly and efficiently using this platform.

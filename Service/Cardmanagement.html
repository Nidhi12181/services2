Issuance:
The process of creating and distributing new cards to customers. This includes generating card numbers, personalizing cards, and delivering them securely.

Activation:
Once issued, the card must be activated by the customer before use. Card management systems ensure secure and seamless activation.

Usage Monitoring:
Tracking card transactions in real-time for purchases, payments, withdrawals, and other activities.

Security and Fraud Prevention:
Implementing measures such as PIN management, chip technology, tokenization, and monitoring for suspicious activities to protect against fraud.

Cardholder Services:
Offering features like balance inquiries, transaction history, and customer support for issues such as lost or stolen cards.

Renewal and Replacement:
Managing card expiration dates, issuing replacement cards, and handling card upgrades.

Deactivation or Blocking:
In cases of loss, theft, or suspected fraud, card management systems allow for quick deactivation to prevent unauthorized use.

Compliance:
Ensuring that the card and its management system adhere to regulations like PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard).
